Theme: Resilience

Sunday's Sermon

Bible Reading
  • Philippians 3:12-14
Sermon Summary

  • 2018 is not over yet!
  • Life is like a roller coaster! There are ups and downs.
  • Sometimes out gifts can put us in trouble.
  • Paul resilient as he was is encouraging to put our gifts into use and push on!
  • God could have used John to do the work that Paul did, but He chose Paul for a reason- Paul had the gift, he had the gift of resilience.
  • Like Paul, it doesnt matter how our past may be, God can pick us up where we are wash us and use us for the greater good!
  • We just need resilience and determination!
  • Paul is saying that he was not going to allow his failures, his misdeeds to hold him back, he was going to be resilient!
  • Sometimes, the things that cause us to cry is what leads us to be triumphant!
  • The example of Joseph- being thrown in the pit, sold to Egypt, placed in prison.
  • Joseph is the "bounce-back king."
  • The very thing that caused him to go through all the troubles is what brought him triumph!
  • The trials we go through come as a test to prepare us for what is to come.
  • We are all wired to win, we have a winning DNA.
  • We should not let circumstances push us down, let's keep pressing on!
  • We don't need a blessing to show off, but a blessing to show up!
  • We don't need a healing to brag about, but one to testify about!

    • This Week's Study

    Prayer Points

    1. How is the Christian life like a race?
    2. What is the value of “pressing on” in our spiritual journey? What is the alternative to pressing on? Which of these options produces spiritual maturity in our lives?
    3. Have you ever been tempted to think that your past victories are enough, so you can stop running towards the goal? What must we do when that tempting thought rises within us?

    Insight Corner

    Action. We will strain towards the goal to win the prize in every area of our lives this week!

    Next Steps

    • We need to reevaluate our opinion of ourselves.
    • We should forget what is behind and strain forward to what is ahead.
    • With God’s help, we will strive to live up to what God has already pronounced us to be in Christ.
